Aurora Borealis

About the Aurora

The aurora borealis, also known as the northern lights, is a spectacular natural light display predominantly seen in high-latitude regions around the Arctic. This phenomenon occurs when charged particles from the sun collide with atoms in Earth's atmosphere, creating beautiful light displays.

Where to See

  • Norway: Tromsø and Svalbard are popular locations for aurora viewing.
  • Canada: Yellowknife and Whitehorse are renowned for their clear skies and frequent sightings.
  • Iceland: Reykjavik offers stunning views and is easily accessible for tourists.

Interesting Facts

  • Auroras occur when solar wind, a stream of charged particles emitted by the sun, collides with Earth’s magnetic field.
  • Colors can vary, including green, pink, red, yellow, blue, and violet, depending on the type of gas and altitude of the collision.
  • The best time to see the aurora borealis is during the winter months, particularly between September and April, when nights are longer.
  • In some cultures, the aurora is considered a mystical phenomenon, often linked to folklore and legends.
